1 Description

[hide]

This script produces a selectable set of masks against the active view: luminance (plus inverted and stretched variants), range masks biased to highlights or shadows, star masks (with and without inversion), a nebula-only mask built from a starless rendition, and a high-pass mask for sharpening localization.

Each mask is created as a fresh image window named <source>_<maskKind> so that multiple masks coexist for comparison.

2 Usage

[hide]

Open from Scripts > EGo > Mask Factory. Pick the source view, check the masks to generate, optionally adjust the high-pass radius, and click Generate.

Source view

Any view, mono or color. The ViewList defaults to the active view.

Luminance variants

Plain luminance, inverted luminance, and stretched luminance (a mid-tones transfer with m = 0.15 for a strong shadow lift).

Range masks (highlights / shadows)

RangeSelection with smoothness 4 from 0.5 to 1.0 for the highlights mask, and from 0.0 to 0.2 for the shadows mask.

Star mask and inverted star mask

StarXTerminator produces a starless rendition; the difference (boosted with mtf 0.25) becomes the star mask. The inverted variant flips it in place. Falls back to the built-in StarMask if StarXT is unavailable.

Nebula-only mask

Luminance of the starless rendition, optionally stretched. Requires StarXTerminator.

High-pass mask

0.5 + (image - gaussian-blurred image). The gaussian sigma is derived from the chosen radius (FWHM / 2.355).
